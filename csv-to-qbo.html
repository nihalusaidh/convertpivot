How to Convert CSV to QBO

Prepare your transactions in a CSV with these columns:

ColumnRequiredDescription
DateYesTransaction date in YYYY-MM-DD format
DescriptionYesPayee or transaction description
AmountYesPositive for credits, negative for debits
TypeNoDEBIT or CREDIT (auto-detected from Amount if omitted)
MemoNoOptional memo text
CheckNumberNoOptional check or reference number

Drop your CSV, click Convert, and download the QBO file ready to import into your accounting software.

Frequently Asked Questions

When migrating between accounting software or uploading transactions from a spreadsheet, QBO format is required by QuickBooks. This converter lets you prepare your data in Excel, then convert to QBO for import.
The CSV should have columns: Date, Description, Amount, Type (DEBIT/CREDIT), and optionally Memo and CheckNumber. Amount can be positive (credit) or negative (debit).
The generated QBO file follows the standard OFX specification and works with any QuickBooks-compatible accounting system.
