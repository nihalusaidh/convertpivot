Frequently Asked Questions

A palindrome is a word, phrase, or sequence that reads the same forwards and backwards. Examples include 'racecar', 'level', and 'A man, a plan, a canal: Panama'. Palindromes can also be numbers like 121 or 12321.
Famous palindromes include 'racecar', 'madam', 'radar', 'refer', 'civic', 'level', and 'kayak'. Famous phrase palindromes include 'A man, a plan, a canal: Panama' and 'Never odd or even'.
A palindromic number reads the same forwards and backwards. Examples include 121, 1331, 12321, and 1234321. Some mathematicians study palindromic primes and palindromic squares.
