How to Convert VCF to Excel (vCard to Spreadsheet)

July 13, 2026

VCF files are how contacts get exported from phones, Gmail, Outlook — basically everything. But opening a VCF file to actually work with your contacts is a pain. They are designed for machines, not people.

When I need to clean up my contacts, remove duplicates, or merge lists from different sources, I always convert VCF to Excel first. In a spreadsheet you can sort, filter, and edit everything at once. Here is how to do it.

Method 1: Using ConvertPivot VCF to XLSX Converter

The fastest method is using the ConvertPivot VCF to XLSX converter. It processes your contact file entirely on your device — no uploads to any server, so your contact data stays private.

Go to the VCF to XLSX page, select your VCF file, and click convert. The tool parses the vCard data and creates an Excel file you can download immediately. Each contact becomes a row, each field becomes a column.

ConvertPivot also offers a VCF to CSV converter if you prefer comma-separated values. Both tools handle vCard versions 2.1, 3.0, and 4.0. For the reverse, use the CSV to VCF converter to turn your spreadsheet back into contact files.

Method 2: Using Google Contacts and Google Sheets

If you use Google services, you can convert VCF to Excel through Google Contacts. This method is free and works well for cleaning up contact data before importing into other applications.

Steps: First, import your VCF file into Google Contacts (contacts.google.com). Click Import in the left sidebar, select your VCF file, and the contacts will be added to your Google Contacts. Then open Google Sheets (sheets.google.com), create a new spreadsheet, go to Extensions > Apps Script to write a simple script that exports contacts, or use the Google Contacts export to CSV option. Google Contacts can export CSV files that open directly in Excel.

The advantage of this method is that Google Contacts handles the complex vCard parsing automatically and handles multiple phone numbers, addresses, and custom fields accurately. The downside is the extra steps compared to a direct converter, especially if you are not already using Google contacts.

Method 3: Using iCloud Contacts (Apple Users)

For Apple users, iCloud provides a convenient bridge between VCF files and spreadsheets. This method works well for contacts from iPhones, Macs, and iPads.

Steps: Go to iCloud.com and sign in with your Apple ID. Click Contacts. Select all contacts (Ctrl+A or Cmd+A). Click the gear icon and select Export vCard to download a VCF of all your iCloud contacts. Then use an online converter like ConvertPivot's VCF to XLSX tool to convert the exported VCF to Excel format. Alternatively, you can select individual contacts and copy/paste relevant fields into a spreadsheet, though this is impractical for large contact lists.

iCloud also supports importing VCF files, so you can merge contact lists from other sources into iCloud first, then export everything as a single VCF for conversion to Excel.

Method 4: Using Email Clients (Outlook, Thunderbird)

Desktop email clients like Microsoft Outlook and Mozilla Thunderbird can import VCF files and export them to CSV or Excel-compatible formats. This is useful for converting contacts from one platform to another.

In Outlook: Import the VCF file into Outlook Contacts (File > Open & Export > Import/Export > Import vCard). Once imported, export the contacts as CSV (File > Open & Export > Import/Export > Export to a file > Comma Separated Values). The resulting CSV file can be opened in Excel and saved as XLSX.

In Thunderbird: Install the ImportExportTools NG add-on, then use it to import VCF files into the Thunderbird address book. The same add-on can export contacts as CSV. This two-step method (import VCF, export CSV) converts contacts efficiently, and the CSV can be opened in Excel for further editing.

Tips for Successful VCF to Excel Conversion

VCF files can vary significantly in structure depending on the source application. iPhone exports often use vCard 3.0 or 4.0 with multiple phone number types (iPhone, mobile, home, work). Android exports may include only basic fields. Gmail exports use vCard 3.0 with thorough field mapping.

When converting, be aware of common issues: multiple phone numbers per contact (work, home, mobile) may each get their own column, duplicate contacts in the source VCF will appear as duplicate rows, special characters (accents, non-Latin scripts) may not display correctly if the encoding is not UTF-8, and photos cannot be preserved in Excel format since XLSX does not natively support image storage in cells.

After conversion, review the spreadsheet for accuracy. Check that names are split correctly into first and last name columns, phone numbers have the correct country codes, email addresses are complete and valid, and all contacts from the original VCF were included in the output.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is a VCF file? It is a contact file format — stores names, phone numbers, email addresses, and other contact info. Phones, Gmail, and Outlook all use it for importing and exporting contacts.
  2. Can I just edit the VCF file directly? Technically yes, it is plain text. But for more than a few contacts, editing raw VCF is tedious and error-prone. Convert to Excel, make your changes, then convert back.
  3. Can I convert multiple VCF files at once? Yes, most online converters handle multiple files. ConvertPivot's VCF tool lets you select several files. For very large batches, combine them into one VCF first.
  4. What fields get preserved? Names, phone numbers, emails, addresses, company info, and job titles. Multiple numbers (work, home, mobile) each get their own column. Photos do not transfer to Excel.
  5. Can I convert Excel back to VCF? Yes. Use ConvertPivot's CSV to VCF tool. Just make sure your columns are mapped to the right fields.
