OFX vs QFX vs QBO — Bank Statement Formats for Accounting

July 13, 2026

If you have ever downloaded bank transactions for accounting software, you have encountered OFX, QFX, and QBO files. These three formats look similar, but they serve different purposes and are tied to specific software ecosystems. Understanding the ofx qfx qbo difference is critical for getting your financial data into Quicken, QuickBooks, GnuCash, or Excel without errors.

This guide explains exactly how these formats differ, which software uses each, and how to convert between them securely.

What Are OFX, QFX, and QBO?

All three formats trace their roots to the Open Financial Exchange (OFX) specification, which was jointly developed by Intuit, Microsoft, and CheckFree in the late 1990s. OFX was designed as an open standard to move financial data securely between banks and personal finance software. Over time, Intuit created two proprietary variants tied to their products.

OFX is the base open standard. QFX is Quicken's extension of OFX. QBO is QuickBooks' extension. Despite different extensions, they all use SGML-based markup with a similar tag structure. This is why a single converter can often handle all three formats.

OFX Format

OFX (Open Financial Exchange) is the vendor-neutral standard for financial data exchange. It is supported by GnuCash, MoneyDance, Moneymanager, and many open-source accounting tools. Most major banks in the US and Europe offer OFX downloads in their online banking portals.

OFX files contain transaction data including dates, amounts, check numbers, payee names, and memo fields. They also include account summary information like opening and closing balances. The format supports credit card, checking, savings, money market, and investment account types.

OFX files are structured as SGML documents with a three-line header followed by tag-based transaction data. Unlike XML, OFX does not require closing tags for all elements, which can cause parsing issues with strict XML parsers. Most modern OFX tools handle both strict and loose markup.

QFX Format

QFX is Quicken's proprietary format derived from OFX. When you download transactions in Quicken directly from your bank via Direct Connect or Web Connect, Quicken saves the data as a QFX file. The format adds Quicken-specific tags for investment transaction types, budget categories, and account grouping.

QFX files are functionally identical to OFX with extended metadata. This means standard OFX parsers can usually read QFX files, though Quicken-specific tags will be ignored. The reverse is not always true — QFX-specific tools may reject plain OFX files that lack the expected proprietary tags.

If you use Quicken, QFX is the recommended download format because it preserves category mappings and investment details that OFX cannot represent.

QBO Format

QBO (QuickBooks Web Connect) is the format used by QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop for importing bank transactions. When you download transactions from your bank's website in QBO format, the file includes mappings to QuickBooks' chart of accounts, tax line assignments, and class tracking fields.

QBO files can be directly imported into QuickBooks via the Banking or Import Data menus. QuickBooks matches downloaded transactions against existing entries for easy reconciliation. The format also supports memorized transactions, payee aliases, and split transactions across multiple accounts.

Most US banks including Bank of America, Chase, Wells Fargo, and Citibank offer QBO downloads alongside OFX and CSV options. QBO is the most convenient format for QuickBooks users because it requires the least manual data mapping.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is an OFX file? OFX (Open Financial Exchange) is an open standard for financial data exchange between banks and accounting software. It supports checking, savings, credit card, and investment accounts with structured transaction data.
  2. What is a QFX file? QFX is Quicken's proprietary extension of OFX. It adds Quicken-specific tags for categories, investment types, and account metadata. QFX files can usually be parsed by standard OFX tools.
  3. What is a QBO file? QBO (QuickBooks Web Connect) is QuickBooks' bank import format. It extends OFX with tags for chart of accounts, tax lines, and class tracking fields for seamless import into QuickBooks.
