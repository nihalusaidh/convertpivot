Top 10 Free Online File Converters in 2026

July 13, 2026

Let us be honest — most "free" online converters are either packed with ads, cap your file sizes at something useless, or upload your documents to some server you will never see again. In 2026, the good ones do things differently: they process everything in your browser, support weird niche formats, and actually respect your privacy.

I have tested dozens of these tools so you do not have to. Here are the top 10 free online file converters ranked by privacy, format support, ease of use, and whether they are actually free — no bait-and-switch paid tiers.

1. ConvertPivot

Best for: Browser-based privacy-first conversions, financial documents, and contact files. ConvertPivot processes all conversions locally in your browser using WebAssembly — no files are ever uploaded to a server. This makes it the most privacy-focused converter available. It specializes in HEIC to JPG, bank statement conversion (PDF to CSV, QBO, OFX, QFX), vCard (VCF to CSV/XLSX), and GPS format conversion (GPX, KML, FIT). All tools are completely free with no file size limits or registration required. Visit ConvertPivot.

2. CloudConvert

Best for: Broadest format support (200+ formats). CloudConvert supports virtually every file format imaginable — images, videos, audio, documents, e-books, archives, and more. It offers fine-grained conversion settings including resolution, bitrate, compression level, and codec selection. The free tier allows 25 conversions per day with a 1 GB file size limit. Files are encrypted during transfer and deleted from servers after 24 hours. CloudConvert has a polished interface and robust API for developers.

3. ILoveIMG / ILovePDF

Best for: Image and document conversion with editing features. The ILove family of tools offers specialized converters for images and PDFs with built-in editing capabilities. ILoveIMG handles JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and SVG conversions with options for resize, crop, and compress. ILovePDF provides PDF to Word, PDF to Excel, merge, split, compress, and OCR features. Both offer free tiers with reasonable limits and are widely trusted for everyday conversion tasks.

4. Zamzar

Best for: Simple, no-frills conversion with email delivery. Zamzar has been a reliable online converter for years. It supports a wide range of formats and sends converted files to your email, which is useful for large conversions that take time. The free tier limits files to 50 MB and 2 conversions per day. Zamzar's interface is straightforward — select file, choose format, enter email — making it accessible for users who prefer simplicity over advanced options.

5. Online-Convert

Best for: Advanced settings and customization. Online-Convert offers detailed configuration options for every conversion type. For video, you can set codec, resolution, frame rate, and bitrate. For audio, you can choose sample rate, channels, and bit depth. For images, you can adjust DPI, quality, and color profile. The free tier supports files up to 100 MB with reasonable conversion speeds. Ad-supported but functional.

6. Convertio

Best for: Cloud integration (Google Drive, Dropbox). Convertio supports converting files directly from cloud storage services including Google Drive, Dropbox, and OneDrive — no download required. It supports over 300 formats and offers a clean, modern interface. The free tier allows 10-minute conversions with a 100 MB file size limit. Convertio is excellent for users who work extensively with cloud storage and need a converter that integrates with their workflow.

7. AConvert

Best for: Document and e-book conversion. AConvert specializes in document, e-book, and archive formats. It supports EPUB, MOBI, AZW3, PDF, DOCX, HTML, and many more. AConvert is particularly useful for converting between e-book formats, with options to customize output margins, fonts, and metadata. Free with no registration — just upload, convert, and download. Simple but effective for specific format niches.

8. FreeConvert

Best for: Large file support on free tier. FreeConvert offers a generous 1 GB file size limit on its free plan without requiring registration. It supports images, video, audio, documents, and archives. FreeConvert provides API access for developers and batch processing capabilities. The service uses secure HTTPS connections and deletes uploaded files within 6 hours. A solid all-purpose converter with competitive free tier limits.

9. YouConvert

Best for: Video and audio conversion with download links. YouConvert focuses on media file conversion with support for all major video and audio formats. It offers presets for different devices (iPhone, Android, PlayStation, Xbox) and platforms (YouTube, Vimeo). The free tier handles files up to 500 MB. Converted files are available for download for 24 hours. Simple interface with clear quality presets makes it accessible for non-technical users.

10. FileZigZag

Best for: Quick conversions without registration. FileZigZag offers a straightforward conversion experience with no signup required. It supports a wide variety of formats including documents, images, audio, video, and compressed archives. The free tier limits files to 50 MB and 2 concurrent conversions. Files are automatically deleted after conversion. FileZigZag is best for occasional quick conversions where you do not want to create an account.

Choosing the Right Converter for Your Needs

The best converter depends on your specific use case. For privacy-sensitive documents like bank statements, contracts, or personal contacts, browser-based local converters like ConvertPivot are the safest choice since your data never leaves your device. For maximum format variety, CloudConvert or Convertio offer the broadest support. For bulk conversions, look for tools with API access or batch processing capabilities.

When evaluating converter tools, consider these factors: Does the tool process files locally or upload to a server? What is the file size limit on the free tier? Are there daily conversion caps? Does the tool support the specific input and output formats you need? What happens to your files after conversion — are they immediately deleted or stored? The answers to these questions help you choose the right tool for each conversion task.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Are free online file converters safe to use? Safety varies by service. The safest converters process files entirely in your browser — your files never leave your device. Always check the privacy policy, look for HTTPS encryption, and avoid uploading sensitive documents to converters that store files on remote servers.
  2. What is the best free online file converter? The best depends on your needs. ConvertPivot specializes in browser-based conversions with strong privacy controls. CloudConvert offers 200+ formats but has file size limits. For simple image conversions, ILoveIMG is user-friendly. For documents, Zamzar is popular.
  3. How do browser-based file converters work? Modern browser-based converters use WebAssembly to run conversion code directly in your browser. When you select a file, it is read into memory, processed by the Wasm module, and the result is offered as a download. The file never leaves your device.
  4. What file types can be converted online for free? Free online converters support images (JPG, PNG, WebP, AVIF, HEIC, GIF, SVG), documents (PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX), audio (MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, OGG), video (MP4, MKV, AVI, MOV), e-books (EPUB, MOBI, PDF), and data files (CSV, JSON, XML, YAML, VCF).
  5. Are there file size limits for free online converters? Yes, most free online converters have file size limits ranging from 50 MB to 2 GB per file on free tiers. Browser-based converters that process files locally may have higher limits since they depend on the device's available memory rather than server capacity.
