How to Convert PDF to Excel for Free

July 13, 2026

I deal with PDF documents all the time — invoices, bank statements, financial reports, you name it. PDFs are fine for sharing but useless when you need to actually crunch the numbers. You want that data in a spreadsheet where you can run formulas, build charts, and filter rows.

Converting PDF to Excel does not have to be complicated or cost anything. I have tried dozens of methods over the years, and here are the ones that actually work without spending a dime.

Method 2: Using Microsoft Excel (Built-in PDF Import)

If you have Microsoft Excel 2013 or later (including Microsoft 365), you can open PDF files directly in Excel without any additional software. This built-in feature uses Power Query to detect and extract tables from PDF documents.

Steps: Open Excel, go to the Data tab, click Get Data > From File > From PDF. Select your PDF file. Excel will analyze the document and show you a navigator panel listing all detected tables. Select the tables you want to import, and click Load. Excel imports the data into your worksheet as a structured table.

This method works well for PDFs with clear, well-structured tables. Complex layouts with merged cells, multi-line entries, or irregular spacing may require cleanup after import. Excel's PDF import is included with all desktop versions — no online subscription required for the basic functionality.

Method 3: Using Google Sheets

Google Sheets offers a free, cloud-based approach to PDF-to-Excel conversion. While Sheets cannot import PDFs directly, you can extract data using the IMPORTDATA function for text-based PDFs or use third-party Google Workspace add-ons for more complex documents.

For simple, text-based PDFs: Copy the text from the PDF and paste it into Google Sheets. Use Google Sheets' Split text to columns feature (Data > Split text to columns) to separate data into columns based on delimiters like tabs, commas, or spaces. This manual approach works for small documents with simple layouts.

For better results, use a dedicated PDF extraction add-on from the Google Workspace Marketplace. Most add-ons offer free tiers with limited conversions per month and paid plans for higher volumes. The advantage of Google Sheets is that your data is automatically saved in the cloud and accessible from any device.

Method 4: Using Free Desktop Software

Several free desktop applications can convert PDF files to Excel format. LibreOffice Calc (part of the LibreOffice suite) includes a PDF import feature that extracts tables from PDF documents. It is completely free, open-source, and runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Steps with LibreOffice: Open LibreOffice Calc, click File > Open, select your PDF file. LibreOffice's PDF import wizard will appear. Configure the import options (which pages to import, how to handle multi-line text), and click OK. The PDF content will be inserted into the spreadsheet, where you can save it as Excel format (XLSX).

The quality of LibreOffice's PDF import depends on the PDF structure. Simple tables with clear borders import well; complex layouts may require manual adjustment. For occasional PDF-to-Excel conversions, LibreOffice provides a capable free alternative to paid software.

Tips for Better PDF to Excel Conversion

Not all PDFs convert equally well. The accuracy of conversion depends on how the PDF was created. PDFs generated from digital sources (like Word documents or web pages saved as PDF) convert much more accurately than scanned documents.

For scanned PDFs, OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is essential. OCR technology analyzes images of text and converts them into machine-readable characters. Many online converters include built-in OCR, but the accuracy depends on scan quality — 300 DPI or higher, clean backgrounds, and clear fonts produce the best results.

After conversion, always review the output for errors. Check that columns aligned correctly, numbers were parsed as numbers (not text), dates are in the correct format, and no data was lost or misaligned. A quick visual scan can catch most conversion issues before you start working with the data.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Can I convert PDF to Excel for free? Yes. ConvertPivot is free, Excel can open PDFs directly, and LibreOffice Calc handles it too. No payment needed.
  2. Does it work with scanned PDFs? It depends on the scan quality. Clean scans at 300 DPI or higher work well. Blurry or handwritten documents are hit or miss.
  3. Why does my converted data look different from the PDF? Excel preserves the data, not the visual layout. Column widths, fonts, and spacing will differ. You might need to adjust formatting after conversion.
  4. Can I convert password-protected PDFs? Most free tools cannot handle password-protected PDFs. Remove the password first or use Adobe Acrobat Pro.
