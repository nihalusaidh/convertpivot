How to Open VCF Files in Excel

July 13, 2026

Ever received a .vcf file and wondered how to actually read it in Excel? You're not alone. VCF (vCard) files are the standard way to share contacts across phones, email clients, and CRMs. But double-click one on your computer and Excel just stares back at you with garbled text. The fix is simple — convert it first.

Can Excel open VCF files directly? Nope. Excel can't parse vCard files directly. You've got to convert that VCF to CSV or XLSX first, then open it. Easy fix.

How do I export contacts from iPhone to Excel? On your iPhone, go to Settings > Contacts > Import from SIM, or use iCloud.com to export contacts as a .vcf file. Then run it through a VCF to CSV converter and open the result in Excel.

What is a vCard format? vCard is the industry standard for storing contact info. A .vcf file is structured text with fields like Name (FN), Phone (TEL), Email (EMAIL), and Address (ADR). There are a few versions (2.1, 3.0, 4.0) with different encoding and field support. Our vCard format guide has the full breakdown.

Can I convert VCF to XLSX directly? Absolutely. The VCF to XLSX converter turns your vCard contacts straight into a native Excel file in one go. No CSV middleman needed.
