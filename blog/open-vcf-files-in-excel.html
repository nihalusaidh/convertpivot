How to Open VCF Files in Excel

July 13, 2026

VCF files (vCard format) are the standard way to store and share contact information across phones, email clients, and CRMs. But when you double-click a .vcf file on your computer, Excel doesn't know what to do with it. You'll see garbled text or an error. This guide explains exactly how to open VCF files in Excel by converting them to a spreadsheet format â€” no advanced skills required.

Can Excel open VCF files directly? No. Excel cannot parse vCard structure directly. You must convert VCF to CSV or XLSX first and then open the converted file in Excel.

How do I export contacts from iPhone to Excel? On your iPhone, go to Settings > Contacts > Import from SIM or use iCloud at iCloud.com to export contacts as a .vcf file. Then use a VCF to CSV converter to convert it to a format Excel can open.

What is a vCard format? vCard is the industry standard for storing contact information. A .vcf file contains structured text with fields like Name (FN), Phone (TEL), Email (EMAIL), and Address (ADR). Versions 2.1, 3.0, and 4.0 differ in encoding and field support. See our vCard format guide for a detailed comparison.

Can I convert VCF to XLSX directly? Yes. The VCF to XLSX converter produces a native Excel file from your vCard contacts in one step, without going through CSV.
