How to Convert MP4 to MP3 Online

July 13, 2026

I regularly extract audio from video files — whether it is a concert video I want as a playlist, a recorded meeting I want to listen to on my commute, or a podcast I want without the video overhead. Whatever your reason, converting MP4 to MP3 is simple and can be done online without installing anything.

MP4 files hold both video and audio in one container. MP3 is just the audio part. Converting means pulling the audio track out and saving it as a compressed audio file. Here are the methods I recommend.

Method 2: Using VLC Media Player (Desktop)

VLC Media Player is a free, open-source media player that includes powerful media conversion capabilities. It supports virtually every audio and video format and can convert between them without additional software. VLC runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Steps in VLC: Open VLC, click Media > Convert/Save. Click Add to select your MP4 file, then click Convert/Save. In the Convert dialog, choose an Audio - MP3 profile. Click Browse to select the output destination and filename, then click Start. VLC extracts the audio track and saves it as an MP3 file.

VLC gives you advanced options for controlling the conversion quality. You can customize the audio bitrate, sample rate, and channel count. For most users, the default MP3 profile produces excellent results. VLC is particularly useful for batch conversions or when you need precise control over output parameters.

Method 3: Using FFmpeg (Command Line)

For users comfortable with the command line, FFmpeg is the most powerful and flexible conversion tool available. It is a free, open-source multimedia framework that can handle any audio or video conversion task. FFmpeg is available for Windows, macOS, and Linux.

The basic command to extract MP3 audio from an MP4 file is: ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -q:a 0 -map a output.mp3. The -q:a 0 flag sets the highest VBR quality (equivalent to approximately 320 kbps CBR). For a specific constant bitrate, use -b:a 192k for 192 kbps or -b:a 320k for 320 kbps.

FFmpeg's power lies in its scriptability and batch processing capabilities. You can write scripts to convert entire directories of video files to MP3, extract audio from specific time ranges, or apply audio filters during conversion. For automated or high-volume conversion workflows, FFmpeg is the tool of choice.

Choosing the Right MP3 Bitrate

The bitrate of your MP3 file determines the balance between audio quality and file size. Here are guidelines for different use cases:

320 kbps (Maximum quality): Use for music you want to keep in your permanent collection, audiophile listening, or when the source audio is high quality. A three-minute song at 320 kbps is approximately 7-8 MB. The difference between 320 kbps and lower bitrates is noticeable on good headphones or speakers.

192 kbps (Balanced quality): Use for everyday listening on mobile devices, workout playlists, or streaming over cellular data. The quality is very good and most listeners cannot distinguish 192 kbps from 320 kbps in casual listening environments. File sizes are about 40% smaller than 320 kbps.

128 kbps (Standard quality): Use for spoken word content like podcasts, audiobooks, lectures, and interviews. The human voice requires less frequency range than music, so lower bitrates are acceptable. A one-hour podcast at 128 kbps is approximately 55-60 MB.

Why Convert MP4 to MP3?

MP3 files are tiny compared to MP4. You can fit thousands of songs on your phone, while the same number of videos would eat up all your storage. MP3 works on every music player, car stereo, and smart speaker out there.

I use it for making playlists from concert videos, saving lectures for offline listening, turning video podcasts into audio-only files, and making ringtones.

  3. How long does conversion take? Usually a few seconds to a minute for most files. Very large videos (over 1 GB) take longer. Browser-based tools are faster for small files since there is no upload step.
  4. What MP3 bitrate should I use for music? 320 kbps for your permanent collection. 192 kbps for everyday listening on your phone. 128 kbps is fine for podcasts and spoken word.
  5. Can I convert MP4 to MP3 on my phone? Yes. Browser-based converters work on iOS and Android without any app installation. I prefer local-processing converters for privacy.
