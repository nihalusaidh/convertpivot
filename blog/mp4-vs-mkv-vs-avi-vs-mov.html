MP4 vs MKV vs AVI vs MOV — Video Format Comparison

July 13, 2026

Video formats are one of those things nobody explains well — so let me just lay it out. Whether you're editing a feature film, uploading to YouTube, or archiving family videos, the choice between MP4, MKV, AVI, and MOV affects compatibility, file size, and quality more than you'd think.

Here's the key thing to understand: all four of these are containers — digital wrappers that hold video, audio, subtitle tracks, and metadata. The actual quality depends on the codec inside, but the container decides where and how that video actually plays. This video format comparison covers everything you need to know.

Frequently Asked Questions

Upload MP4 with H.264 video and AAC audio. That's what YouTube recommends for the best balance of quality and processing speed.
MP4 works everywhere — phones, TVs, browsers, game consoles. MKV is built for power users: unlimited audio tracks, subtitles, chapters, and any codec you want. Use MP4 for sharing, MKV for archiving.
You can, but you probably shouldn't. AVI lacks modern codecs, subtitle support, and has a 2 GB file limit. Convert AVI to MP4 and you'll get a smaller file that actually plays on modern devices.
Pick MOV if you're editing video professionally — it handles higher bit depths, alpha channels, and uncompressed audio. Pick MP4 if you're sharing the final video with anyone else.
Most smart TVs don't support MKV natively. MKV is an open-source container that TV manufacturers rarely license. Convert MKV to MP4 for TV playback, or use a media player like VLC or Plex.
