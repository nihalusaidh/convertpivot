Top 10 Free Online File Converters in 2026 — Compared and Reviewed

July 16, 2026

Every week someone asks me the same question: what's the best free online file converter? The short answer? There isn't one. The right tool depends on what you're converting, how big the files are, how much you care about privacy, and whether you need batch processing. I've spent the last few months testing every free converter I could find. Here's what I learned.

What Makes a Good File Converter?

Before we dive in, let's talk about what actually matters. Privacy is the biggest one. Some converters process files on your device in the browser. Others upload your files to a server. If you're converting something sensitive — bank statements, legal contracts, personal photos — you want a client-side converter. Speed also varies wildly depending on the format and file size. Format support is huge too. A converter that only handles images is useless when you need to convert a video. And don't forget about file size limits, daily conversion caps, and whether you need to sign up for an account.

1. ConvertPivot

Full disclosure — I built ConvertPivot. The whole thing runs on client-side JavaScript. You drag a file onto the page, it gets read into your browser's memory, converted with libraries like PDF.js and pdf-lib, and then downloaded. Your file never touches a server. That makes it the best option for privacy-sensitive stuff like bank statements, legal docs, or personal photos. The catch? Really large files can crash your browser tab, and there's no batch processing yet. Format support covers the most common needs: PDF, images, audio, documents, and data formats.

2. CloudConvert

If you need a ton of format options, CloudConvert is your best bet. They support over 200 formats including weird ones like DXF, ODP, and PPM. The free tier gives you 25 minutes of conversion time per day, which is plenty for occasional use. Files get uploaded to their servers and deleted after conversion. They've got a clear privacy policy and GDPR compliance. The downsides are the 25-minute daily cap and the 1 GB file size limit on the free plan.

3. Zamzar

Zamzar's been around since 2006 and supports over 1100 format conversions. You upload a file, enter your email, and get a download link. The free tier limits you to 2 conversions per day and 50 MB per file. Having to use your email is annoying, but it does mean you can convert on one device and download on another. Zamzar really shines for obscure format pairs that other converters miss.

4. Online-Convert

Think of Online-Convert as a Swiss Army knife. It handles images, video, audio, documents, ebooks, and even has tools for hashing and encoding. The free tier has a 100 MB limit and queues can be slow. The interface is cluttered with ads, but the conversion quality is solid. You also get optional settings like resolution, bitrate, and compression level for each conversion.

5. FreeConvert

FreeConvert lives up to its name — unlimited conversions with a 1 GB file size limit, no account needed. The interface is clean and conversion speed is above average. They cover images, video, audio, and documents. The one catch? Free users see ads and get slower queue priority, but files still convert in reasonable time. If you need batch processing, this is one of the free options that actually supports it.

6. iLovePDF

iLovePDF is all about PDFs, and nothing but PDFs. Merge, split, compress, convert, edit — they do it all. The free tier handles files up to 100 MB with unlimited conversions. The interface is polished and the tools work consistently. Files are uploaded to their servers and deleted after a few hours. For PDF-specific work, it's hard to beat.

7. Smallpdf

Smallpdf is another PDF-focused tool with a clean design. The free tier limits you to 2 files per hour, which is pretty restrictive for anything beyond occasional use. The paid plan removes that and adds OCR and e-signatures. Where Smallpdf stands out is output quality — their PDF compression, in particular, produces smaller files than most competitors without visible quality loss.

8. PDF24

PDF24 offers both a desktop app and a web version. The web version is completely free with no file size limit and no daily cap. The desktop version runs locally, so nothing leaves your computer. PDF24 covers all the common PDF operations plus some extras like PDF protection and PDF analysis. For heavy PDF users, this is the most generous free option out there.

9. ImageMagick (Online)

ImageMagick is the engine behind a lot of image converters, but you can use it directly through online wrappers too. It supports over 200 image formats, including esoteric ones like FITS, SGI, and XBM. You get fine-grained control over compression, color depth, and processing options. The online interfaces are usually ugly, but the conversion quality is best in class for images.

10. Convertio

Convertio supports over 300 formats covering documents, images, video, audio, ebooks, and archives. The free tier limits you to 10 conversions per month and 100 MB per file. It works well for occasional use, but the monthly cap makes it impractical for regular work. The interface is modern and the conversion quality is good.

Privacy-Focused Alternatives

If privacy is your top concern, stick with client-side converters like ConvertPivot or desktop software like PDF24 and HandBrake. Server-based converters inevitably have access to your files, even if they promise to delete them afterward. The only way to be absolutely sure your files aren't accessed is to never send them anywhere. Client-side processing guarantees this — the file is read into memory, converted with JavaScript libraries, and discarded when you close the tab.

When to Use Each Converter

Use ConvertPivot for bank statements, legal documents, or personal photos where privacy matters. Use CloudConvert for format conversions ConvertPivot doesn't support, especially video and audio. Use Zamzar for obscure format pairs nothing else handles. Use iLovePDF or PDF24 if you work with PDFs daily. Use FreeConvert for batch image conversions. Use Convertio as a last resort when nothing else works.

Final Thoughts

The online converter scene in 2026 is pretty healthy. There are good free options for every type of conversion. The main trade-off is always privacy versus convenience. Server-side converters offer more formats and handle larger files, but your data leaves your device. Client-side converters keep your files private but have more limited capabilities. Pick the tool that fits your specific need, and keep a backup for edge cases.
