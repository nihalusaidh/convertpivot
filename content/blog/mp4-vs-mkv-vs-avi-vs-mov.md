Video file formats can be confusing. With dozens of containers and codecs available, choosing the right format for your project matters more than most people realize. Whether you are editing a feature film, uploading to YouTube, or simply archiving your home videos, the choice between MP4, MKV, AVI, and MOV directly affects compatibility, file size, and quality.

Each of these four formats is a container — a digital wrapper that holds video, audio, subtitle tracks, and metadata. The quality of the video depends on the codec inside the container, but the container determines where and how that video can play. This **video format comparison** breaks down everything you need to know about MP4 vs MKV vs AVI vs MOV.


Which format should I use for YouTube? ▼Upload MP4 with H.264 video and AAC audio. That is what YouTube recommends for the best balance of quality and processing speed.What is the real difference between MP4 and MKV? ▼MP4 works everywhere — phones, TVs, browsers, game consoles. MKV is built for power users: unlimited audio tracks, subtitles, chapters, and any codec you want. Use MP4 for sharing, MKV for archiving.Can I still use AVI files? ▼You can, but you probably should not. AVI lacks modern codecs, subtitle support, and has a 2 GB file limit. Convert AVI to MP4 and you will get a smaller file that actually plays on modern devices.When should I pick MOV over MP4? ▼Pick MOV if you are editing video professionally — it handles higher bit depths, alpha channels, and uncompressed audio. Pick MP4 if you are sharing the final video with anyone else.Why will not my MKV files play on my smart TV? ▼Most smart TVs do not support MKV natively. MKV is an open-source container that TV manufacturers rarely license. Convert MKV to MP4 for TV playback, or use a media player like VLC or Plex.
