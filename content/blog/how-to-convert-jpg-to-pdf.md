I convert JPG images to PDF all the time — for printing, combining scanned pages into a single document, or sending a portfolio to a client. Doing it in your browser is fast, free, and needs no software.

PDF keeps everything looking the same on any device. Unlike a JPG that someone might accidentally crop or resize, a PDF is locked. Here are the best ways to do it.


Can I convert JPG to PDF for free? ▼Yes. ConvertPivot and most online tools are completely free. No watermarks, no signup, no limits.Can I put multiple JPGs into one PDF? ▼Yes. Select all the images you want, arrange them in order, and the converter creates a multi-page PDF. Great for photo albums or scanned documents.Does converting to PDF reduce image quality? ▼No. The original JPG data is embedded in the PDF without recompression. The image looks exactly the same.Can I convert HEIC (iPhone) photos to PDF? ▼Yes. ConvertPivot has a dedicated HEIC to PDF converter that does it in one step. No need to convert to JPG first.What page size should I use? ▼Letter or A4 for documents. Auto-fit for photos. Landscape 16:9 for presentations. Most converters let you pick.
