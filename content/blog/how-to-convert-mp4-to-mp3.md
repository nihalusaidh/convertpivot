I regularly extract audio from video files — whether it is a concert video I want as a playlist, a recorded meeting I want to listen to on my commute, or a podcast I want without the video overhead. Whatever your reason, converting MP4 to MP3 is simple and can be done online without installing anything.

MP4 files hold both video and audio in one container. MP3 is just the audio part. Converting means pulling the audio track out and saving it as a compressed audio file. Here are the methods I recommend.


Is it legal to convert MP4 to MP3? ▼If you own the content — your own videos, purchased music, royalty-free stuff — then yes. Downloading copyrighted music from YouTube without permission is not legal.Does converting lose audio quality? ▼It depends on the bitrate. 320 kbps is near-perfect. 192 kbps is fine for most listening. You cannot get better quality than the original source.How long does conversion take? ▼Usually a few seconds to a minute for most files. Very large videos (over 1 GB) take longer. Browser-based tools are faster for small files since there is no upload step.What MP3 bitrate should I use for music? ▼320 kbps for your permanent collection. 192 kbps for everyday listening on your phone. 128 kbps is fine for podcasts and spoken word.Can I convert MP4 to MP3 on my phone? ▼Yes. Browser-based converters work on iOS and Android without any app installation. I prefer local-processing converters for privacy.
