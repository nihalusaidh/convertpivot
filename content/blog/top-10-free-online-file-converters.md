Let us be honest — most "free" online converters are either packed with ads, cap your file sizes at something useless, or upload your documents to some server you will never see again. In 2026, the good ones do things differently: they process everything in your browser, support weird niche formats, and actually respect your privacy.

I have tested dozens of these tools so you do not have to. Here are the top 10 free online file converters ranked by privacy, format support, ease of use, and whether they are actually free — no bait-and-switch paid tiers.


Are free online file converters safe to use? ▼Safety varies by service. The safest converters process files entirely in your browser — your files never leave your device. Always check the privacy policy, look for HTTPS encryption, and avoid uploading sensitive documents to converters that store files on remote servers.What is the best free online file converter? ▼The best depends on your needs. ConvertPivot specializes in browser-based conversions with strong privacy controls. CloudConvert offers 200+ formats but has file size limits. For simple image conversions, ILoveIMG is user-friendly. For documents, Zamzar is popular.How do browser-based file converters work? ▼Modern browser-based converters use WebAssembly to run conversion code directly in your browser. When you select a file, it is read into memory, processed by the Wasm module, and the result is offered as a download. The file never leaves your device.What file types can be converted online for free? ▼Free online converters support images (JPG, PNG, WebP, AVIF, HEIC, GIF, SVG), documents (PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX), audio (MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, OGG), video (MP4, MKV, AVI, MOV), e-books (EPUB, MOBI, PDF), and data files (CSV, JSON, XML, YAML, VCF).Are there file size limits for free online converters? ▼Yes, most free online converters have file size limits ranging from 50 MB to 2 GB per file on free tiers. Browser-based converters that process files locally may have higher limits since they depend on the device's available memory rather than server capacity.
