Why Convert Financial Files Privately?

Financial documents contain some of your most sensitive personal information — account numbers, transaction histories, balances, and sometimes your full identity details. Uploading bank statements or financial export files to a server-based converter puts your data at risk of breaches, unauthorized access, or misuse. ConvertPivot runs all conversion entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript, meaning your files never leave your computer. There are no servers processing your data, no uploads, and no copies stored anywhere.

This browser-first approach is especially critical for financial data because bank statements and export files (OFX, QFX, QBO) often contain enough information for identity theft if intercepted. By keeping processing local, you maintain complete control over your financial information. Whether you're reconciling accounts for a small business, preparing tax documents, or simply organizing personal finances, you can use our tools with confidence knowing your data never leaves your machine.

Frequently Asked Questions

What financial formats are supported?

We support OFX, QFX, QBO, PDF bank statements, and CSV formats. You can convert between any of these formats — OFX to CSV, QFX to CSV, QBO to CSV, CSV to QBO, CSV to OFX, CSV to QFX, PDF bank statement to CSV, and more. We also offer bank-specific converters for Chase, Wells Fargo, and Bank of America.

Is my financial data safe?

Yes. All file conversion happens entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your financial data never leaves your device — it is never uploaded to a server. We have no access to your bank statements, transaction data, or personal financial information. You can even use the tools offline.

Do you support my bank?

If your bank provides downloadable statements in PDF, OFX, QFX, or QBO format, our converters will handle them. We also offer dedicated converters for major banks including Chase, Wells Fargo, and Bank of America that are optimized for their specific statement layouts. For other banks, try our universal PDF bank statement converter.

Can I edit transactions before converting?

Our converters parse and structure your transaction data automatically. For editing transaction data, we recommend converting to CSV first, editing the CSV in any spreadsheet application (Excel, Google Sheets), and then converting back to your desired format using our CSV to QBO, CSV to OFX, or CSV to QFX tools.
