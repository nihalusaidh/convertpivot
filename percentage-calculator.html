Frequently Asked Questions

To calculate what percent X is of Y, use the formula: (X / Y) × 100. For example, 25 out of 200 is (25/200) × 100 = 12.5%.
Percentage change measures how much a value has increased or decreased relative to its original value. The formula is: ((New - Original) / Original) × 100. A positive result is an increase, negative is a decrease.
To calculate a percentage increase, use: New Value = Original × (1 + Percentage/100). For example, a 20% increase of 50 is 50 × 1.2 = 60.
