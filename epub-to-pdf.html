Why Convert EPUB to PDF?

EPUB is the standard reflowable format for eBooks, but PDF is often preferred for printing, sharing with non-technical users, archiving documents with fixed layout, or submitting manuscripts. Converting EPUB to PDF gives you a paginated, print-ready document that looks identical on every device. How EPUB to PDF Conversion Works

The conversion process is entirely client-side. First, JSZip extracts the EPUB archive to reveal its internal structure. The tool reads the META-INF/container.xml file to find the OPF (Open Packaging Format) file, which contains the book's metadata, manifest (list of all files), and spine (reading order of content documents). Each XHTML chapter is then rendered in a sandboxed iframe with the EPUB's original CSS applied. html2canvas captures each page-sized chunk of the rendered content, and jsPDF assembles these images into a downloadable PDF document. Because everything runs in your browser, there are no file size limits beyond what your device can handle, and your privacy is fully protected.

Benefits of Converting EPUB to PDF

Universal Compatibility: PDF files open on virtually any device without special software. This makes PDF the preferred format for professional document sharing, manuscript submissions, and academic distribution.

You can print individual chapters or the entire book with consistent margins, headers, and pagination. Academic publishers and self-published authors often require PDF submissions because the layout is predictable and professional.

During conversion, these styles are applied to the rendered content so the PDF closely matches the original eBook's visual design. Limitations of EPUB to PDF Conversion

Layout Differences: Because EPUB is reflowable and PDF is fixed-layout, the pagination in the converted PDF will differ from how the same content would appear in an e-reader. Line breaks, page breaks, and spacing are determined by the browser's rendering engine at the chosen page dimensions, not by the original EPUB's intended layout. Fixed-layout EPUBs (often used for children's books or comics) may not convert accurately because they rely on absolute positioning.

CSS Complexity: Some EPUBs use complex CSS with media queries, custom fonts, or advanced selectors that may not render identically in a browser context. The tool applies the EPUB's stylesheets but cannot guarantee pixel-perfect reproduction. EPUBs with heavy JavaScript-based interactivity or embedded video will not capture that functionality in the PDF â€” those elements are static in the output.

Font Availability: If the EPUB embeds custom fonts via @font-face, those fonts are not loaded during the browser-based rendering. The PDF will use system fallback fonts, which may alter the appearance slightly. For most eBooks, the difference is minimal and the content remains fully readable.

When to Use EPUB to PDF Conversion

Ideal for: Manuscript submission to publishers or literary agents, printing textbook chapters for study, creating archival copies of eBooks, sharing content with non-technical users, converting reference materials for offline access, preparing court documents or legal references that require fixed pagination.

Not ideal for: Preserving EPUB-specific features like reflowable text or adjustable fonts, DRM-protected EPUBs that require decryption, interactive eBooks with embedded multimedia content, or fixed-layout graphic novels that rely on precise pixel positioning across spreads.
