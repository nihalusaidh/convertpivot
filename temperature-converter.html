Frequently Asked Questions

To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, use the formula: F = (C × 9/5) + 32. For example, 30°C = (30 × 9/5) + 32 = 86°F.
Absolute zero is the lowest possible temperature where no thermal energy remains. It is 0 Kelvin (−273.15°C or −459.67°F). At this point, all molecular motion stops.
Different temperature scales exist for historical and practical reasons. Celsius is used worldwide for everyday measurements, Fahrenheit is common in the US, and Kelvin is the scientific standard based on absolute zero.
