How to Convert WebP to JPG

WebP is Google's image format designed for the modern web. It delivers excellent compression — typically 25-35% smaller files than JPEG at equivalent quality. However, many desktop applications, email clients, and photo editing tools still lack WebP support.

Our converter uses the browser's built-in WebP decoder to render your image at full resolution, then exports it as a standard JPEG file. The process preserves the original dimensions, color profile, and quality. Images with transparency will have a white background applied since JPEG does not support alpha channels.

This is especially useful when downloading images from modern websites that serve WebP by default and you need to use them in documents, presentations, or share with others.
