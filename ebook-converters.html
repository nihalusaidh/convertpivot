Understanding E-book Formats

EPUB is the open standard format for e-books, supported by almost all e-readers except Amazon Kindle. EPUB files are ZIP archives containing HTML, CSS, and images — making them versatile and reflowable. Converting EPUB to PDF creates a fixed-layout version ideal for printing or sharing with people who don't have an e-reader.

PDF (Portable Document Format) preserves exact layouts, fonts, and styling. It is the best choice for documents with complex formatting, tables, or images that must appear in a specific position. PDF to EPUB conversion enables reflowable reading on phones and tablets.

CBZ (Comic Book ZIP) is an archive format for comics and manga. Each page is stored as an image file inside a ZIP container. Converting CBZ to PDF creates a single document with all pages in order, suitable for printing or reading on any PDF-capable device.

When to Use Each Format

Use EPUB for reading on phones, tablets, and most e-readers (except Kindle). Use PDF for fixed-layout documents, academic papers, and printing. Use CBZ for comic collections and manga. Use TXT for the simplest possible format — pure text with no styling.
